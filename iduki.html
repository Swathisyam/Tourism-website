Idukki

Idukki district lies amid the Western Ghats of Kerala. Though it is regarded as the second-largest district in the region, it has the lowest population density among the districts of Kerala, the urban population being higher than the rural. Idukki is rich in forests and also known as the "Spice Garden of Kerala".Rugged mountains and forests cover about 97 percent of the total area of the district. The district consists of five taluks: Thodupuzha, Devikulam, Idukki, Udumbanchola, and Peerumedu. The Munnar Hills seems to be bathed in a dreamy shade of blue with the blooming of Neelakurinji (strobilanthes kunthiana) once in every 12 years, as the flower, rarest of the rare, does not grow in any other part of the world. some important destinations listed:

Idukki Arch dam reservoir

Idukki Arch Dam situated between Kuravan and Kurathi mountains is the biggest dam in Kerala. It is the main source of hydroelectric power generation in Kerala. It's Arch shape is a unique construction in order to grab the gift of the nature.
