Thiruvananthapuram

Kerala state capital-Thiruvananthapuram is situated along the Arabian Sea with isolated hills on a coastal plain spreading over an area of 250 sq. km. Located at an average elevation of about 16 feet above the sea level. Two major rivers flow through the city: river Karamana and the Killi.Besides being the political nerve centre of the state and home to a number of Government offices (both central and state), companies and organizations,Thiruvananthapuram also houses many premier educational institutions including the Kerala University.Thiruvananthapuram is also home to Indiaâ€™s first IT park, Technopark, which was established in 1995. Major Tourist Attractions

Napier Museum

The museum houses a rare collection of archaeological and historic artifacts, bronze idols, ancient ornaments, a temple chariot and ivory carvings. It also contains the Sree Chitra Art Gallery, which contains works from Raja Ravi Varma and Nicholas Roerich, as well as examples of Mughal and Tanjore art.

Ponmudi hills

Ponmudi (the Golden Peak) is a hill station is a part of the Western Ghats mountain range that runs parallel to the Arabian Sea. Also called Kashmir of Kerala

Kovalam Beach

Internationally renowned beach with three adjacent crescent beaches- Lighthouse Beach, Hawah Beach, Samudra Beach
